Mar 4, 2024 — Claude 3 family announced: **Haiku, Sonnet, Opus** (ascending capability). Opus and Sonnet immediately available on claude.ai and the Claude API (now generally available in 159 countries); Haiku coming soon.

**Capabilities.**
- Opus leads on MMLU, GPQA, GSM8K, near-human comprehension/fluency.
- All three improved on analysis/forecasting, nuanced writing, code, non-English (Spanish/Japanese/French).
- **Vision capabilities** on par with leading models — first vision-capable Claude. Important for enterprises with up to 50% of knowledge bases in PDFs/charts/slides.
- **Fewer refusals** — significantly less likely to refuse benign prompts that border guardrails.
- **Improved accuracy** — Opus is 2× more accurate than Claude 2.1 on hard open-ended factual questions; admits uncertainty rather than hallucinating. Citations coming soon.
- **200K context window** at launch; all three models capable of 1M+ tokens (available to select customers). Opus achieved >99% needle-in-a-haystack recall; in some cases recognized that the needle was artificially inserted.

**Speed/cost.** Sonnet 2× faster than Claude 2/2.1 with higher intelligence. Opus same speed as Claude 2 with much higher intelligence. Haiku reads a 10K-token research paper with charts in <3 seconds.

**Safety.** ASL-2 per Responsible Scaling Policy. Less biased than prior models on BBQ benchmark. Red-teaming finds negligible catastrophic risk.

The Claude 3 family marked Anthropic's transition to a tiered family architecture (Haiku/Sonnet/Opus) that persisted through Claude 4 and beyond.
