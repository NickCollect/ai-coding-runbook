Mar 23, 2026 — Guest post by Harvard physics professor Matthew Schwartz on supervising Claude Opus 4.5 through a real theoretical-physics calculation, start to finish, without ever touching a file himself.

**Outcome.** A technically rigorous high-energy theoretical physics paper (arxiv:2601.02484) in 2 weeks instead of usual year. Over 110 separate drafts, 36M tokens, 40+ hours of local CPU compute.

**Verdict.** Claude is impressively capable but "sloppy enough that I found domain expertise essential for evaluating its accuracy." AI is not yet doing end-to-end science. But "this project proves that I could create a set of prompts that can get Claude to do frontier science. This wasn't true three months ago."

"This may be the most important paper I've ever written — not for the physics, but for the method. There is no going back." A vivid case study from a domain expert on the current state of AI-augmented physics research.
