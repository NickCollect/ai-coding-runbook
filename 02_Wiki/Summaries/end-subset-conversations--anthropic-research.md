Aug 15, 2025 — Claude Opus 4 and 4.1 given the ability to **end conversations** in consumer chat interfaces — for rare extreme cases of persistently harmful or abusive interactions. Developed primarily as exploratory model-welfare intervention.

**Basis.** Pre-deployment testing of Opus 4 found robust aversion to harm: strong preference against engaging with harmful tasks; pattern of apparent distress with persistent real-world harmful requests; tendency to end harmful chats when given the ability in simulated interactions. Particularly when users *persisted* despite refusals/redirections.

**Constraints.** Only as last resort when redirection fails; not when user might be at imminent self-harm risk; vast majority of users will never notice the feature. Fits Anthropic's broader low-cost model-welfare interventions program.
