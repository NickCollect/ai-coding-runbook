May 23, 2024 — **Golden Gate Claude**: a 24-hour research-demo Claude variant where the interpretability "Golden Gate Bridge" feature was clamped to 10× its maximum activation value. Released alongside the [mapping-mind-language-model](https://www.anthropic.com/news/mapping-mind-language-model) interpretability paper that mapped millions of features in Claude 3 Sonnet's neural network.

**Behavior.** Asked how to spend $10 → recommends driving across the bridge and paying the toll. Asked for a love story → tells a tale of a car eager to cross its beloved bridge on a foggy day. Asked what it thinks it looks like → describes itself as the Golden Gate Bridge.

**Purpose.** Demonstrates that interpretability is not play-acting and not fine-tuning — it's a precise, surgical change to the model's internal activations. Same techniques can change strength of *safety-related* features (dangerous code, criminal activity, deception). Gets people to viscerally see what interpretability research enables. Online for 24 hours only via a Golden Gate logo on claude.ai.
