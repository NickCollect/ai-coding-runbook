Feb 15, 2023 — Tested hypothesis that RLHF-trained LLMs can **morally self-correct** when instructed. Found strong evidence across three experiments. Capability for moral self-correction emerges at **22B parameters**, typically improves with scale and RLHF training. At this scale, models obtain (1) instruction-following capability and (2) understanding of complex normative concepts of harm (stereotyping, bias, discrimination). Cause for cautious optimism that LLMs can be trained to abide by ethical principles.
