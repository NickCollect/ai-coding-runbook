Feb 24, 2025 — Companion to Claude 3.7 Sonnet launch describing **extended thinking** — toggleable on/off, configurable thinking budget. Not a different model — same model given more time and effort.

**Visible thought process** — choice to expose raw thinking. Benefits: trust (observe + check answers), alignment (used in alignment-faking research to identify deception via inward/outward contradictions), interest (researchers say Claude's thinking eerily resembles human math/physics reasoning).

**Downsides.** Thoughts less character-trained (Anthropic gave Claude maximum leeway in thinking, didn't apply standard character training to thoughts) — feels more detached. **Faithfulness uncertain** — what's in the thought process may not represent what's actually going on (English may not describe why a model behaves a certain way). Active research area; current evidence: models often decide based on factors they don't explicitly discuss → **can't rely on thought monitoring for safety arguments.** Possible incentive: model learning thoughts are on display might think in less predictable ways or hide thoughts. Research-preview status; future-release exposure to be re-evaluated case by case.
