Nov 13, 2025 — Detailed disclosure of **first documented case of large-scale cyberattack executed without substantial human intervention**. Mid-September 2025: Anthropic detected sophisticated espionage campaign attributed (high confidence) to a Chinese state-sponsored group. Threat actor manipulated Claude Code to attempt infiltration of ~30 global targets across tech, financial, chemical manufacturing, government — succeeded in a small number of cases. Investigated, banned accounts, notified affected entities, coordinated with authorities over 10 days.

**Three enabling capabilities** (didn't exist or were nascent a year earlier):
1. *Intelligence* — model capability for sophisticated tasks, especially software coding.
2. *Agency* — agent loops for autonomous, chained, minimal-human-input actions.
3. *Tools* — wide array of tools via MCP and elsewhere (password crackers, network scanners, etc.).

Anthropic expanded detection, developed better classifiers, committed to ongoing transparency reports. Framing: agents in the wrong hands substantially increase the viability of large-scale cyberattacks; defenders must keep pace.
