Feb 24, 2026 — **RSP v3.0** released. Refined after 2+ years of implementation. Reinforces what worked, improves what didn't, adds measures for transparency/accountability.

**Theory of change as articulated in this version:**
- *Internal forcing function* — RSP makes safeguards a launch requirement, not a wishlist.
- *Race to the top* — induce other labs and policymakers to adopt similar policies.
- *Consensus-building at thresholds* — capability thresholds become moments to push from unilateral (Anthropic) to multilateral (industry, governments) action.
- *Looking to future* — by later thresholds (e.g., state-actor robust safeguards), Anthropic expects need to coordinate with governments since unilateral implementation will be infeasible.

V3 incorporates lessons from when capabilities arrived faster than expected (web browsing, code, computer use, agency) — each new capability brings new risk class. Continues conditional/if-then commitment structure, with refined ASL-2/3 detail and intentionally less-detailed ASL-4+ to be filled in as evidence accumulates.
