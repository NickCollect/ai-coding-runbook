Jan 29, 2026 — Randomized controlled trial (52 mostly-junior software engineers using Python ≥1×/week for over a year, unfamiliar with the Trio async library). Two coding features to build using Trio; quiz on concepts immediately after. AI-assistant arm vs hand-coded arm.

**Results.**
- AI assistance led to **statistically significant 17% lower quiz scores** (~2 letter grades) vs coded-by-hand. Concepts used minutes earlier weren't retained.
- Time saved with AI was small and not statistically significant.
- **How AI was used mattered.** Strongest mastery came from participants who used AI to build comprehension — asking follow-up questions, requesting explanations, posing conceptual questions while coding independently.

**Implication.** Cognitive offloading via AI may undermine skill development even as it speeds work. Important for AI product design (build for learning), workplace AI policy, and societal resilience as humans must still catch errors and provide oversight.
