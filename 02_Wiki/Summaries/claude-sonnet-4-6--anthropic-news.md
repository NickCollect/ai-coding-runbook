Claude Sonnet 4.6 launched Feb 17, 2026. Most capable Sonnet model — full upgrade across coding, computer-use, long-context reasoning, agent planning, knowledge work, design. **1M-token context window in beta.** Default model in claude.ai and Claude Cowork for Free/Pro users. Pricing unchanged from Sonnet 4.5 at $3/$15 per M tokens.

**Coding.** Early-access developers preferred Sonnet 4.6 over Sonnet 4.5 by a wide margin; often even preferred over Opus 4.5 (the Nov 2025 Opus model). Performance previously requiring Opus-class is now within Sonnet reach. In Claude Code, users preferred Sonnet 4.6 over Sonnet 4.5 ~70% of the time, and over Opus 4.5 59% of the time — rated less prone to overengineering and "laziness," better at instruction following, fewer false claims of success, fewer hallucinations.

**Computer use** — major leap on OSWorld (16 months of steady gains). Anthropic introduced general-purpose computer-use Oct 2024. Sonnet 4.6 also substantially more resistant to prompt injection (similar to Opus 4.6). Anthropic released [Claude for Chrome](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-for-chrome) extension to use this capability.

**1M context** — holds entire codebases, long contracts, dozens of papers. Sonnet 4.6 *reasons effectively* across that context. On **Vending-Bench Arena** (multi-AI competition for simulated business profit), Sonnet 4.6 invested heavily in capacity for first 10 simulated months, then sharply pivoted to profitability — finished well ahead.

**Safety.** As safe or safer than recent Claude models. Safety researchers describe its character as "broadly warm, honest, prosocial, and at times funny... very strong safety behaviors, no signs of major concerns around high-stakes forms of misalignment."

**Customer reports.** Databricks: matches Opus 4.6 on OfficeQA. Replit: "extraordinary" performance-to-cost ratio. Cursor: "notable improvement over Sonnet 4.5 across the board." GitHub: strong resolution rates for searching across large codebases. Cognition: closed gap with Opus on bug detection. Front-end code and financial analysis stand out; visual outputs notably more polished (layouts, animations, design sensibility).
