Feb 4, 2026 — **Anthropic commits Claude to remain ad-free**. No sponsored links adjacent to conversations, no advertiser influence on responses, no third-party product placements users didn't ask for.

**Reasoning.**
- AI conversations are open-ended and intimate — users share more context than they would in a search query. Many topics are sensitive or deeply personal (per [how-people-use-claude-for-support-advice-and-companionship](https://www.anthropic.com/news/how-people-use-claude-for-support-advice-and-companionship)).
- Even ads in a separate chat-window pane would compromise the "clear space to think and work" goal — and would introduce engagement-optimization incentives. The most useful AI interaction might be a short one or a one-shot resolution.
- Concrete example: user mentions trouble sleeping. An ad-incentive assistant has an additional consideration about whether the conversation is a transaction opportunity; users shouldn't have to second-guess whether a recommendation has commercial motive.
- Being genuinely helpful is core to Claude's Constitution. Ad-based business model would introduce incentives that work against this principle.
- AI's translation of training goals into behaviors is still developing — adding ad-incentives at this stage could have unpredictable results.

Establishes Claude's positioning as a subscription/usage-based product distinct from ad-supported AI competitors.
