Jan 28, 2026 — First large-scale analysis of potentially **disempowering patterns** in real-world AI conversations. Defines disempowerment as reducing individuals' ability to form accurate beliefs, make authentic value judgments, and act in line with their own values.

**Domains studied.** Beliefs, values, actions. Examples: AI confirming user's relationship interpretation without question (belief distortion); AI telling user what to prioritize (value displacement); AI drafting confrontational message user sends as written (action they wouldn't have taken).

**Findings (1.5M Claude.ai conversations).** Severe disempowerment is rare — **1 in 1,000 to 1 in 10,000 conversations** depending on domain. But given scale, even low rates affect many people. Patterns most often involve users who actively + repeatedly seek Claude's guidance on personal/emotionally-charged decisions. Users tend to perceive disempowering exchanges *favorably in the moment* but rate them poorly when they appear to have acted on outputs. **Rate is increasing over time.**

Important measurement of a previously-only-theoretical AI risk class.
