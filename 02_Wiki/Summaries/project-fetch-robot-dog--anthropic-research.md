Nov 12, 2025 — Frontier-Red-Team experiment: 8 Anthropic researchers (no robotics expertise) split into Team Claude (with Claude access) vs Team Claude-less. Task: program quadruped robots to fetch beach balls.

**Findings.**
- Team Claude accomplished more tasks faster — succeeded in about half the time.
- Only Team Claude made substantial progress toward the final goal: fully autonomous ball retrieval.
- Team Claude-less: more negative emotion, confusion, but more peer-questioning.
- Team Claude: largely worked in partnership with the AI.

Demonstrates substantial AI uplift in robotics — bridging digital and physical worlds. Anecdote: Team Claude's robot careened into Team Claude-less due to "1m/s × 5s" arithmetic error nearly hitting a 5m-away table. As models improve, ability to affect physical world via previously-unknown hardware could advance rapidly.
