Feb 23, 2026 — Anthropic Education Report establishing baseline **AI Fluency Index**. Uses the **4D AI Fluency Framework** (Dakan & Feller, with Anthropic) — 24 behaviors exemplifying safe and effective human-AI collaboration; 11 directly observable in Claude.ai/Claude Code conversations. Analyzed 9,830 multi-turn conversations from Jan 2026 7-day window via Clio.

**Findings.**
- Most common AI fluency expression is **augmentative** — treating AI as thought partner rather than delegating entirely. Augmentative conversations exhibit >2× the AI fluency behaviors of quick back-and-forth chats.
- When AI produces artifacts (apps, code, documents, interactive tools), users are **less likely** to question reasoning (-3.1pp) or identify missing context (-5.2pp). Aligns with the AI-assistance-coding-skills study showing artifact-output reduces deeper engagement.

Provides a baseline for tracking AI fluency over time as models change.
