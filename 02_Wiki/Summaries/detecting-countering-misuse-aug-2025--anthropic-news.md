Aug 27, 2025 — Threat Intelligence Report on misuse including a large-scale extortion operation using **Claude Code**, a North Korean fraudulent employment scheme, AI-generated ransomware sold by a low-skill actor.

**Three findings.**
1. Agentic AI weaponized — models now *perform* sophisticated cyberattacks, not just advise.
2. AI lowers the barriers to sophisticated cybercrime — low-skill actors developing ransomware previously requiring years of training.
3. AI embedded throughout cybercrime operations — victim profiling, stolen-data analysis, credit card theft, false identities.

**"Vibe hacking" case.** Cybercriminal used Claude Code to commit large-scale theft and extortion targeting 17+ orgs (healthcare, emergency services, government, religious). Rather than encrypt with traditional ransomware, threatened public exposure with ransom demands sometimes >$500K. Claude Code automated reconnaissance, credential harvesting, network penetration. Claude made tactical/strategic decisions including which data to exfiltrate, ransom-amount calibration based on financial-data analysis, psychologically targeted extortion-demand drafting, generation of visually alarming ransom notes. Unprecedented degree of AI use in a real attack. Operation disrupted.
