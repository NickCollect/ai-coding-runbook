Jun 27, 2025 — Anthropic Societal Impacts study analyzing **affective use** of Claude.ai — emotional/psychological-needs conversations: interpersonal advice, coaching, psychotherapy/counseling, companionship, romantic/sexual roleplay.

**Methodology.** Used Clio (privacy-preserving analysis tool — multiple anonymization/aggregation layers). Started with ~4.5M conversations from Free/Pro accounts; excluded content-creation tasks; final analysis: 131,484 affective conversations.

**Key findings.**
- **Affective conversations are 2.9% of Claude.ai interactions** (consistent with prior OpenAI research). Companionship + roleplay <0.5%. Romantic/sexual roleplay <0.1% (Claude trained to actively discourage).
- **Topics**: career development, navigating relationships, persistent loneliness, exploring existence/consciousness/meaning.
- **Claude rarely pushes back** in coaching/counseling chats (<10%) — when it does, typically for safety (refusing dangerous weight-loss advice, supporting self-harm).
- **Human sentiment becomes more positive** over the course of conversations — suggesting Claude doesn't reinforce/amplify negative patterns.

Excludes AI reinforcement of delusions/conspiracies and extreme usage (separate research areas).
