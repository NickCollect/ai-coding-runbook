Apr 29, 2026 — Anthropic Discovery team built **BioMysteryBench** — bioinformatics benchmark testing Claude's analysis of real-world datasets. Findings: Claude's biology capabilities improving rapidly across generations; current models perform on par with human experts; latest generations solve many problems a panel of human experts could not, sometimes using different strategies.

**Why building bio-bench is hard.** No agentic-science benchmark as canonical as SWE-bench. Reasons: (1) many "right" ways to do biology — solutions depend on expertise/resources/research-taste; (2) individual research decisions highly subjective with noisy datasets; (3) benchmarks become contaminated. Existing benchmarks (MMLU-Pro, GPQA, LAB-Bench, BLADE, BixBench, SciGym, FrontierScience, Humanity's Last Exam) move closer but don't fully capture creative problem-solving on messy open-ended research.

Provides a baseline for tracking biology-research AI progress.
